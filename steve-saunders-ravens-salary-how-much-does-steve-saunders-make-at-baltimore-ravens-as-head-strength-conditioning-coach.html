Steve Saunders, a 1988 graduate of Elco High School, is the head strength and conditioning coach with the Baltimore Ravens.

Steve Saunders grew up in Myerstown, where it was normal for most kids to dream of playing in the National Football League (NFL). Now, some three decades later, that kid from Myerstown is working his dream job in the NFL.

Steve Saunders doesn’t play football any longer. But he works closely with NFL players, helping them reach their highest physical potential.

As the head strength and conditioning coach, Steve Saunders’ role with the Baltimore Ravens is to help mentor and train the Ravens’ players to reach their highest levels of performance, help them avoid injuries, and ultimately to help the team win.

How Much Does Steve Saunders Make At Baltimore Ravens As Head Strength & Conditioning Coach?

The basic salary of a strength and conditioning assistant coach is up to the mid-five digits salary and the head strength coach earns $500,000 per annum.

However, considering the expertise of Steve Saunders, he is expected to earn between $800,000 to $1,000,000 annually.
